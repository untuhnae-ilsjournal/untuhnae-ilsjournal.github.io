    Preface - An Overview of the Venture

    The main purpose of this journal is to document Group V's iLS Group Project in General Chemistry 2 and General Physics 2. The goal of the project is to create a Pepper's Ghost Illusion Machine. Let's look at what defines Peppers Ghost Illusion for more context. When a real or recorded image is reflected at a 45-degree angle in a transparent screen, viewers see a reflected virtual image that appears to have depth and appear out of nowhere in a Pepper's Ghost Illusion. The Pepper's Ghost Illusion is still used in amusement parks, theaters, and other locations. Show below is how the pepper’s ghost machine works.

    While not a true hologram, this Pepper's Ghost activity requires much less technology and is a good exploration of light and viewing angles. Real holograms are three-dimensional images created by the interference of light beams. They should be viewable from any angle and allow the viewer to see the object from all sides. Real holograms can be seen without the use of specialized lenses, screens, or other devices. For our project, we incorporated this illusion into a device that will be displayed at the end of the school year during our iLS Expo.
