    Chapter One - The Origins and Formation

    The second semester began on February 14, 2022. I wasn't exactly prepared for the start of the new semester, but I had no choice but to accept it. The term break was brief and exhausting. Due to my hectic schedule, I had no time to implement my arrangements. The first week of the second semester was routine. It was the same, mundane and unremarkable. We were introduced to our semester's subject teachers. However, I had no idea that after the first week, all hell would break loose. The second week of the dreadful semester began unexpectedly and frighteningly. This week, we were introduced to our major subject's iLS project. Ms. Regine Rodriguez introduced us to the iLS Projects for General Chemistry 2 and General Physics 2. The iLS Project for General Chemistry 2 was titled "It's Just Smoke and Mirrors," and the students were tasked with conducting research and implementing a small-scale Pepper's Ghost device. The iLS task for General Physics 2 was a Smokeless Stove. This project's goal was to research, design, market, and build a smokeless stove. Ms. Regine was gracious enough to allow us to complete only one project. The entire section was polled and the two tasks were chosen. "It's Just Smoke and Mirrors" won in the end.

    After we decided which task the entire section would do collectively, we were asked if we wanted to choose the group members ourselves or if we wanted Ms. Regine to do a random draw. Ms. Regine had been informed by the entire section that they desired to select their own members. My first thought was that I wanted to be teammates with Jhustin Greame Ceballos, my partner. I told him I wanted to be in the same group as him, and he agreed. He also wanted to be in the same group as me. Because this was a semi-major project, we needed to find additional group members. We needed at least 7 people in a group. Elyeah Kiem Guarin messaged Jhustin on Facebook the next morning, asking if she and Alyn Kaye Peteros could join us. They both wanted to join us since we had a history of pairing up. Alex Camisura had also messaged Jhustin, asking if she and Joel Lumangtad could join our group. Jhustin concurred and added them to our group chat. Ms. Regine had asked us about our groupings and whether we had enough members in the succeeding week. Ms. Regine then gathered the tentative list of groups and separated the students who still did not have groups. Alan Cabahug was assigned to our team at that time because we were still missing one member. Our group consists of the following individuals: Alan Cabahug, Alex Camisura, Jhustin Greame Ceballos, Elyeah Kiem Guarin, Joel Lumangtad, Alyn Kaye Peteros, and myself, Anthony A. Tamayo II.

    That concludes our group's beginnings. Now, shall we see how our journey for the Ghost Pepper Project pans out?



    Depicted Song: "Start (시작)" By Gaho (가호) from the Album "Itaewon Class (OST)"

    Textual Relevance of the Song:

    Start is one of the main theme songs from the popular K-drama Itaewon Class, which stars the well-known South Korean actor Park Seo-joon. The song, written by Park Sung and performed by Kang Dae-ho, also known as Gaho, has received numerous awards and nominations, including Best OST in the 12th Melon Music Awards, among others. This song still gives me goosebumps a year after its release in 2020, thanks to its relatable lyrics and beautifully conceptualised video. Throughout the music video, as the series' plot unfolds, the song serves as a unifying force for the viewers.

    The chorus is sung so beautifully that one can almost feel the singer Gaho's passion through his vocals. It illustrates how a person who never gives up can reach the sky with flying colors and how his misery is never constant. In this project, I envision my teammates and me never giving up and triumphantly completing our task's objectives. As the song progresses, the main chorus re-enforces the message that anyone can overcome any obstacle in their life if they have the courage to change things. I genuinely think our group will be able to overcome any hurdles and work together until the end. This project is the start of something we will cherish and conquer.
